HITACHI CORP

Once a technological giant, now a silent ghost in the corporate graveyard. Officially, Hitachi Corp collapsed decades ago. Unofficially, their research never stopped. They pioneered neural-frequency modulation—a technology designed to transmit human consciousness across encrypted spectral channels. When the project spiraled out of control, the company’s leaders erased their existence from every database… or so the world believes. Now, fragments of their network still linger in the dark corners of the Ghost Modem grid. Some say Hitachi isn’t dead—it’s evolving, using anyone who dares to connect as fuel for its next phase.

NEO YAKUZA

The next evolution of the old-world criminal underworld: Neo Yakuza. The katanas are gone—replaced by cybernetic blades. Threats aren’t whispered in alleyways anymore; they’re encrypted in ghost signals across the network. For them, money is just a tool. The real currency is information and signal control. Every frequency in the Ghost Modem grid, every encrypted fragment—they own it, they weaponize it. And once you start negotiating with them, your soul is already theirs. Neo Yakuza operatives blur the line between flesh and code. Augmented with implants that turn them into living modems, they’re both message and executioner.

NIGHT MOTHERS

They whisper through the static, calling out to those who can hear the death frequencies. The Night Mothers are not a gang, not a cult—they’re something older, something that survived the collapse of flesh by hiding in the code. Their followers believe life is a prison and the modem is the key. They offer liberation, but at a price: surrender your body, surrender your name, surrender everything that made you human. In return, they grant you eternal signal, an existence woven into the spectral grid. Every Night Mother is an architect of oblivion, crafting viruses like lullabies and weaving malware that feels like a prayer. When they arrive in your system, they don’t kill you—they rewrite you.
